About the Typeface

Poppins is a geometric sans-serif typeface designed by the Indian Type Foundry. It features clean, minimalist letterforms with rounded shapes and a modern, versatile look, making it suitable for both digital and print applications. Poppins comes in multiple weights, from thin to extra bold, allowing for a wide range of typographic uses. It is especially popular in web design due to its legibility and aesthetic compatibility with modern interfaces. The typeface supports many languages, including those that use the Devanagari script, enhancing its global usability.

In addition to its geometric precision, Poppins stands out for its balanced proportions and monolinear strokes, which contribute to its contemporary appeal. Its versatility makes it suitable for a wide range of projects, from corporate branding to editorial layouts, and it pairs well with serif fonts for a clean and modern contrast. Poppins also supports extensive character sets, including ligatures and diacritics, making it an excellent choice for multilingual projects. Its open-source availability via Google Fonts has further contributed to its widespread use across websites and digital platforms, offering designers a professional yet accessible typographic option.
